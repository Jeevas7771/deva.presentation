• PROBLEM SECTION



    • Malaysia's Ringgit is experiencing depreciation due to internal and external factors such as economic instability, reduced foreign investments, and global market trends. The decline weakens the purchasing power of citizens and increases the cost of imports, creating challenges for businesses and households.


What Is Internal Factors?


Internal factors are influences within an entity that affect its performance, such as employee skills and financial resources.​


Factor One

Economic Instability : Malaysia relies heavily on exports of commodities like palm oil and crude oil, which are subject to global price fluctuations. Any dip in demand or price for these products directly impacts the country's revenue, weakening the currency.


Factor Two

High Public Debt : Malaysia's public debt, currently over 60% of GDP, reduces investor confidence and limits fiscal space for economic recovery.

Justification

Economic Instability : Malaysia has been facing challenges like slower GDP growth and reduced consumer spending. For example, while inflation in Malaysia has been relatively controlled, concerns about insufficient domestic economic reforms have led to less investor confidence, reducing the flow of capital into the country. What Is External Factors?


External factors are influences outside an entity that impact its performance or decisions, such as market trends and government regulations.​


Factor One

US Dollar : The US Federal Reserve’s interest rate hikes in 2023-2024 caused global capital outflows from emerging markets like Malaysia. Investors shifted to US bonds, reducing demand for the Ringgit.​

Factor Two

China's Economic Slowdown : As a major trading partner, China's reduced economic growth affects Malaysia's exports. For example, lower Chinese demand for palm oil and rubber hurt export revenue.

Justification

Strength of the US Dollar: The US dollar has been strengthening due to sustained high interest rates by the US Federal Reserve. This attracts global investments into dollar-denominated assets, reducing the demand for other currencies, including the Ringgit​CLICK HERE
