Boosting Foreign Investment


Example : With the Ringgit weakening, importing goods such as machinery, electronics, and raw materials becomes more expensive. This increases production costs for businesses and raises prices for consumers.



Improving Export Competitiveness


Example : If Malaysia focuses on producing higher-quality palm oil and expands into new markets, foreign buyers will need Ringgit to purchase it, boosting demand for the currency.



Diversifying the Economy


Example : Malaysia could invest in the tech sector, such as building a startup ecosystem. More local startups means higher demand for Ringgit from investors, which can help stabilize the currency.



Monetary Policy Adjustments


Example : If Bank Negara Malaysia raises interest rates, foreign investors may move money into Malaysia's savings accounts to earn higher returns, which would increase demand for the Ringgit and help its value rise.
